Campus Life

Justice Knowledge Love (JKL)

Recognized for it's positive, student-centered approach to teaching and learning, AIS values the sense of community within the school. Our school emblem, the Eagle, is a universal symbol of strength, endurance, and pride that unites everyone in our community under the motto: Justice, Knowledge, and Love.

Swimming Pool

This is the AIS swimming pool. Students learn / practice their swimming techniques during Physical Education period. The front side is shallow and the back side is deep. Ususallly, the front is used for beginners and the back is used for intermediate / advanced. Students play many fun activites in the pool such as basketball and volleyball.

Basketball Court / Classrooms

This is where the students spend their reccess. Here, students can play basketball, volleyball, and soccer. Students can also spend this time gathering with their friends. After their recess, students head to their classrooms which are above the court. Students also have lockers, where they keep their belongings throughout the day.

School Achievements

TEDx American International School HK
TEDx American International School HK

TEDx American International School HK was once again held virtually with the event theme of Unmasked. Our 8 speakers, ranging from sixth to twelfth grade, unveiled their ideas worth spreading and voiced their true thoughts on them. All speakers had participated and worked hard throughout the second and third trimester in the TED-ED Club to develop their talks.

AIS Middle School Samba Band
AIS Middle School Samba Band

The symbolism of a mask can be interpreted in many different ways, but for some, it means keeping things hidden, inaccessible, and isolated. This translates to the recent experience of the AIS Middle School Samba Band, as an isolated existence caused by a lockdown resulted in the inaccessibility to communal music-making. And yet now, as the band comes together in more favorable circumstances, these limitations are removed and the collaborative force of drumming can be heard pulsing through the AIS campus once more.
